Authoring Guidelines

This page explains the structure of the sales modules and the creative guidelines the AI follows when generating new content. Our goal is to create content that is clear, consistent, and effective.

Module Structure

Each module is a JSON file with two main sections: metadata and content. The metadata contains information about the module, while the content section holds the outreach materials.

Metadata

The metadata section includes:

  • module_id: A unique identifier for the module (e.g., "AI-A1").
  • title: The title of the module (e.g., "Data & AI Practice Overview").
  • module_type: The type of module, such as "Core Capability" or "Success Story".
  • practice_area: The relevant practice area, like "AI & Data".
  • relevant_roles: A list of job titles the module is intended for.
  • overview: A brief summary of the module's purpose.

Content

The content section is divided by outreach channel:

  • email: Includes a subject line and body text for an email.
  • phone: Provides a phone script with an opening, key message, evidence point, and voicemail.
  • linkedin: Contains a connection request and an InMessage draft.
  • objections: A list of common prospect objections and effective responses.
  • collateral: A link to any relevant case studies or whitepapers.

AI Authoring Guidelines

The AI is given a set of core principles to follow when generating module content:

Persona-Centric

Content is tailored to the specific pain points and priorities of the target audience.

Value-Oriented

The focus is on the value and outcomes of our services, not just the features.

Evidence-Based

Claims are supported by quantifiable metrics and real-world client examples.

Clear and Concise

The language is professional, direct, and easy to understand.

Technology-Agnostic

We emphasize our expertise across multiple platforms, not just one specific technology.

Action-Oriented

Every piece of content guides the prospect toward a clear and logical next step.
